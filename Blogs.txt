An introduction to the terrifyingly wonderful world of technology.

Modern technology is terrifying. From self-driving cars taking people 
from A to B, to the rise of AI. The world of technology is evolving at 
a rate we cannot even comprehend.

The world we live in is nothing like before. We have access to all the 
information we could want - yet some of us cannot use the brain power to 
write an essay. We are the most technologically advanced generation - yet 
this presents a monumental fear of unemployment.

Technology is amazing. I certainly love to code, and build circuits. I 
love to create electronic gifts or mechanical toys. I love to build things 
to develop my skills. However, I am aware that technology has myriad 
negatives. I believe we need to have an intelligent conversation to ensure 
the safety and security of future generations without getting rid of this 
marvel altogether.

If these are ideas that interest you - keep an eye on this blog. I discuss 
my own takes on both the faults and benefits of modern technology.

                        AI & The Environment
                            15-06-2026
===================================================================
A deep dive into the facts - Both the problems, but also the reality.

Today, the most  topical subject is Artificial Intelligence without a doubt.
Between the ethics of using generative AI, to the envrionmental impact of data
centres - people are arguing daily about AI.

However, with peoples identities being shaped around whether or not they are
"pro-AI" or "anti-AI" - facts are lost.
Don't get me wrong, I find GenAI terrifying - the fact it can write faster than
me, often times better. The fact it can produce pieces of "art" in meere 
miliseconds. The fact GenAI has been used to target vulnerable groups with 
blackmail and manipulation.

There is no ambiguity - AI has evils that must be assessed and destroyed.

However, the topic of this blog is a seperate "issue" - Artificial Intelligence 
and our plannet. I have yet to witness a day in the era of AI where someone is 
not spouting misinformation about its impact on the Environment. Tiktok and 
instagram have all collectively decided that the AI industry is the sole reason 
we will undergo water shortage. 

Figures such as 560 billion litres of water consumed by global data centres per 
annum may not be factually incorrect - however, they are most certainly misleading.

Humans don't understand numbers as stand alone items - we need a comparison to 
understand the actual impact of this consumption. For example, the global fashion
industry consumes at least 80 Trillion liters of water annualy. That is more than
140 times as much as the current AI industry, and is still 9 times more than global 
projections for AI at its peak.  

Of course, two wrongs do not make a right - but to blame a scarcity of water on AI 
is just wrong. IoT Technology is generally the least of our concerns with respect to 
the Environment. Even when IoT uses up carbon emissions, it often reaches a point
of being a net positive environmentally. For example, people being able to work from 
home has reduced global emissions, despite the fact that they require wireless networks 
to do so.

AI has yet to provide a positive in the workforce - and perhaps it won't. However,
to claim AI is going to kill our planet ignores the real industries that are killing
the planet. You cant put all your energy into a pickpocket when there are serial killers
walking around - and focusing on AI instead of fast fashion or the meat industry is doing
that for the environment.

There are most certainly valid conversations about AI that must be had - however, it's 
environmental concerns are definitely the bottom of that list.
